For years my writing lived on other people's platforms — Medium, Hashnode, Substack. They're great for reach, but the words, the layout, and the distribution were never fully mine. So I'm bringing it home.

This is the first post hosted directly on my own site.

## Why own the platform

- **The words are mine.** No paywalls I didn't choose, no layout I can't control.
- **One canonical home.** Everything I write — and everything I've written elsewhere — is indexed here in one place.
- **It compounds.** A personal site you keep writing on becomes an asset that grows quietly in the background.

## How this blog works

It's deliberately simple and has no backend:

1. I write a Markdown file in `blog/posts/`.
2. I add one entry to `blog/posts.json`.
3. The site renders it into a real, shareable page.

External posts live in the same list and link out — so readers get the full picture in one feed.
